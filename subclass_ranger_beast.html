Ranger's Companion Features

Ranger's Companion (3rd Level)

Feature Details
Beast Companion You gain a beast companion that is trained to fight alongside you. Choose a beast with no larger than Medium size and a challenge rating of 1/4 or lower. The beast obeys your commands and can use actions like Attack, Dash, Disengage, or Help on your turn. The beast's hit point maximum equals its normal maximum or four times your ranger level, whichever is higher.
Beast's Abilities The beast gains proficiency with your proficiency bonus applied to AC, attack rolls, damage rolls, saving throws, and skills. It can also spend Hit Dice during short rests.
Autonomous Action If you are incapacitated or absent, the beast acts on its own, protecting both you and itself. It never requires your command to use its reaction, such as making an opportunity attack.
Beast Death If the beast dies, you can bond with another beast after spending 8 hours performing a magical bonding ritual with a non-hostile creature.

Exceptional Training (7th Level)

Feature Details
Bonus Action Command If your beast companion doesn’t attack on your turn, you can use a bonus action to command it to take the Dash, Disengage, or Help action on its turn.
Magical Attacks The beast’s attacks now count as magical, overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

Bestial Fury (11th Level)

Feature Details
Multiple Attacks When you command your beast companion to take the Attack action, it can make two attacks, or it can use the Multiattack action if it has that feature.

Share Spells (15th Level)

Feature Details
Spell Sharing When you cast a spell targeting yourself, you can also affect your beast companion if it is within 30 feet of you.
