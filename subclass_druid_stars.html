Circle of Stars Druid

Feature Description
Star Map At 2nd level, you create a star chart that serves as a spellcasting focus. You gain the Guidance cantrip and the Guiding Bolt spell, which doesn't count against your prepared spells. You can cast Guiding Bolt a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses after a long rest. If lost, you can recreate it through a 1-hour ceremony during a short or long rest.
Starry Form At 2nd level, you can use Wild Shape to take on a starry form. The form sheds light, lasts for 10 minutes, and offers different benefits based on the constellation you choose: Archer (radiant damage), Chalice (healing), and Dragon (improved concentration checks).
Cosmic Omen At 6th level, you can consult your Star Map for omens after a long rest. Depending on whether you roll an even or odd number, you gain access to Weal (add a d6 to a creature’s roll) or Woe (subtract a d6 from a creature’s roll). You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining uses after a long rest.
Twinkling Constellations At 10th level, your Starry Form improves. The damage/healing from Archer and Chalice becomes 2d8, and while in the Dragon form, you gain a flying speed of 20 feet and can hover. You can also change your constellation at the start of each turn.
Full of Stars At 14th level, while in Starry Form, you become partially incorporeal, granting you resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.
